Sea Bunny

Jorunna parva, or Sea Bunny, is a species of dorid nudibranch. Its black-and-white rhinophores somewhat resemble a rabbit's ears. The papillae, used for sesnory functions, give it the appearance of a furry animal.

Spanish Dancer

Spanish Dancer Nudibranches can be found in the Indo-Pacific where they live near rocky shelters and coral reefs. Spanish dancers are divisive in the world of sea slugs, eliciting awe and disgust in equal measure. While some liken their appearance to the frills of a flamenco dancer's skirt, others compare them to the sole of a badly-worn foot.

Spanish Shawl

These colorful nudibranches live off the Pacific Coast of North America. The neon orange appendages on their back are the cerata which extract oxygen from the sea water. Scientists think their gills are orange so they can camouflage with their prey. When threatened, they gracefully move away by flexing their body and flashing their bright gills.
